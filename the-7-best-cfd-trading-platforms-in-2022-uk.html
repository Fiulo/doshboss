What are CFDs?
Contracts for difference, commonly known as CFDs are unregulated financial contracts that allow traders to speculate on the price movement of a financial asset without actually having to own that asset.
CFDs are sometimes described as ‘virtual shares’ because they are not owned by the trader, but rather the value of the CFD contract is based on the value of the underlying shares at the time the contract is created.
The most common derivative of a financial asset is the futures contract. This is based on a future trading date and is the reverse of the CFD. Futures contracts are more popular among professional traders who use them as a benchmarking tool or to hedge against a position.
Risk warning: CFDs are complex instruments and come with a high risk of losing money rapidly due to leverage.
